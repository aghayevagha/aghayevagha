About Me

I am a research assistant at Azerbaijan Technical University under the supervision of Dr. Yadigar Imamverdiyev, and adjunct instructor at BHOS, Baku, Azerbaijan. Aside from that, I also have experience as a machine learning engineer in industry, with a closer focus on satellite imagery.
My primary research interests include cryptography: with a particular interest in lattice-based cryptography and homomorphic encryption for privacy-preserving technologies. I have done my thesis in exploring lattice-based Identity-based encryption under supervision of Dr. Arkady Yerukhimovich , where I was an active participant of his crypto reading club at GWU. I have worked on several projects on applications of approximate homomorphic encryption for machine learning and shape matching algorithms. I am currently working on attacking multiple-key NTRU problem using Grobner basis computation.

Publications

Privacy-Preserving Shape Matching with Leveled Homomorphic Encryption (preprint)

Aghayev, A., & Imamverdiyev, Y. (2025). Cryptology ePrint Archive.

Abstract: We introduce privacy-preserving efficient 2D Fourier Shape Matching algorithm with CKKS scheme. The algorithm efficiently compares the difference of Fourier Shape Descriptors of two input shapes in encrypted domain, using SIMD operations and approximate arithmetic.
